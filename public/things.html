History, wonder & a little mischief

Things to Do

Three hundred years of history you can actually walk through.

The Lighthouse
Built in 1872, still operational. Climb 129 cast-iron spiral steps for a panoramic view of Jekyll Island, Brunswick, and the Atlantic. The adjacent Maritime Museum covers WWII U-boat activity just offshore.
Christ Church & Cemetery
Built in 1884 on ground where the Wesley brothers preached in 1735. My parents are buried here. Bring the family. Spend a quiet hour. It matters.
Fort Frederica
National Monument. General Oglethorpe's 1736 fort where the Battle of Bloody Marsh ended Spanish ambitions for good. Real ruins, real history, no museum ropes.
Driftwood Beach, Jekyll Island
Ancient bleached trees rising from the sand on Jekyll's northern tip. One of the most photographed beaches in the Southeast. Go at low tide. Go at sunset. Go twice.
Georgia Sea Turtle Center
The only sea turtle rehab hospital in Georgia, on Jekyll Island. Tour the facility, meet recovering turtles, and if timing is right — watch a release. May through August for nesting season walks.
The Pier at Night
Putt-putt, live music, ice cream, sunset over St. Simons Sound. The Pier is one of only three in Georgia that extends into the Atlantic. The best place to be after dinner.
