Protected Forever

No Development. On Purpose.

Georgia state law strictly limits development on the island. Combine that with the St. Simons Land Trust — which has preserved over 1,300 acres — and you get an island that genuinely looks like it did when you were a kid. Tall live oaks draped in Spanish moss line every road. No neon signs. No traffic jams.

🚲
Island Life

Leave Your Keys in the Car

This is genuinely one of those places. People wave. Strangers say hello. You can bike everywhere — to the beach, to dinner, to the pier — and nobody locks their door. It's the kind of easy Southern living that feels increasingly rare, and makes you want to stay forever.

⛵
Golden Isles

Five Islands, One Unforgettable Place

St. Simons is the anchor of Georgia's Golden Isles — a constellation that includes Sea Island, Jekyll Island, Little St. Simons, and the port city of Brunswick. Each has its own personality, and all are within easy reach by bike, car, or boat.

📖

A Place That Has Made Its Mark in History

From the flags of five nations to WWII German U-boat watches in the Atlantic, St. Simons has been at the center of American history for nearly 300 years. Fort Frederica — a National Monument — was built in 1736 by General James Oglethorpe to defend Georgia against the Spanish. Christ Church, where John and Charles Wesley once held services under oak trees, still stands today. The Lighthouse, built in 1872, still guides mariners. History isn't in a museum case here. You walk through it on a Tuesday morning bike ride.

1736 — Fort Frederica Founded

General James Oglethorpe builds a fort to defend Georgia against the Spanish. Today it's a National Monument — ruins you can walk through with your kids.

1735 — John & Charles Wesley

The Wesley brothers hold services under the live oaks. The present Christ Church, built in 1884, stands on the same ground. The Bias family has deep ties here.

1872 — The Lighthouse

The 104-foot St. Simons Island Lighthouse is built. Still operational. Climb all 129 steps for a panoramic view of Jekyll Island, the mainland, and the Atlantic Ocean.

1928 — Sea Island's Cloister Opens

The iconic Cloister opens on neighboring Sea Island. It will go on to host presidents, world leaders, and generations of the Bias family's neighbors.

Every Summer Since

The Bias family has been coming back since before memory. The trees haven't changed. The tides haven't changed. That's the magic of St. Simons Island.

East Beach

Where to Stay

On St. Simons Island, location is everything — and East Beach is the sweet spot. Central to the grocery store, the Pier, the beach, and the best restaurants, with some of the most beautiful private homes on the island.

🌊

East Beach: The Bias Family Headquarters

Near the historic Coast Guard Station on the southeastern end of the island, East Beach sits at the center of everything. The beach is wide and uncrowded. The homes here are some of the nicest on the island — many with private pools, right on or near the water. It's quiet, low-traffic, and positioned perfectly between the grocery store and the Pier. This is where the family stays.

🏠
Best Area

East Beach Vacation Rentals

Look for homes near the Coast Guard Station. Private pools are common, and the best properties are just a short walk or quick bike ride to the beach. Search VRBO or Airbnb and filter for East Beach or "East End" for the best options.

📍
Location Win

Everything Within Easy Reach

From East Beach you can bike to the Pier (about 10 minutes), reach the grocery store easily, and access East Beach itself on foot. No car required for most of your favorite things — which is very much the point.

⭐
Luxury Option

The King and Prince Resort

A historic St. Simons resort on the ocean, within easy reach of East Beach. Has its own spa. Pools, oceanfront rooms, and a restaurant with a good view. A solid option if you'd prefer a hotel over a rental home.

Local Flavors

Food & Entertainment

St. Simons dining is the real deal — no tourist trap chains to wade through. Fresh-caught seafood, legendary BBQ, a steakhouse where history was made, and nightly live music under the Georgia sky.

🦀
Seafood

The Crab Trap

A St. Simons institution. Fresh local seafood, great views, and the kind of laid-back atmosphere that makes you realize you should have ordered more. Come hungry.

🍺
Fun & Casual

Brogen's

The quintessential St. Simons hangout at the Pier. Cold drinks, good food, a covered outdoor deck, and live music most nights. If you're looking for the post-beach crowd, they're here.

🦪
Fresh & Local

Wolf Island Oyster Company

Local oysters, coastal cuisine, and the freshest catch done simply and well. Named after one of the Golden Isles' most pristine wildlife refuges — and the food lives up to the setting.

🥩
Family Legacy

Bennie's Red Barn

A St. Simons steakhouse with a story. This is where the Bias parents had their first date. Rustic, warm, and genuinely excellent. Steak done the old Southern way. Going here isn't just dinner — it's a family tradition.

☀️
Morning Ritual

Sweet Mama's

The best breakfast and brunch on the island. Lines form for a reason. Get here early, order the specials, and plan a slow morning around it. This is the kind of place that sets the tone for a perfect island day.

🔥
As Seen on TV

Southern Soul Barbecue

Featured on a national TV BBQ show — and it deserves every bit of the attention. Slow-smoked, no-frills, soul-deep Georgia barbecue. Located near Redfern Village. If you haven't been, this one's mandatory.

🎵

Nightly Live Music

One of St. Simons' best-kept secrets: live music almost every evening. The Pier area and Redfern Village both host regular performers — local and regional acts playing under the Georgia stars. It's the perfect end to a day on the island. Grab a drink, find a spot on the waterfront, and let the music carry you.

History, Nature & Adventure

Things to Do & Visit

From a 150-year-old lighthouse to a WWII museum, from Sea Island's billionaire estates to sea turtle releases at sunset — the Golden Isles offer more things to do than any single trip can hold.

🏛️
Iconic Landmark

St. Simons Lighthouse & WWII Museum

Built in 1872 and still active. Climb 129 cast-iron spiral steps for panoramic views of Jekyll Island and the Georgia coast. The adjacent Maritime Museum covers the island's history from Native Americans through World War II, including the U-boat attacks just offshore.

⛪
Family & Heritage

Christ Church & Cemetery

Built in 1884 on the same ground where John & Charles Wesley held services in 1735. One of the most serene and historically significant spots on the island. The cemetery holds many of the island's oldest families — including members of the Bias family. A deeply personal stop.

🏰
National Monument

Fort Frederica

A National Park Service site — the ruins of General Oglethorpe's 1736 fort. It's where the Battle of Bloody Marsh was fought in 1742, turning back the Spanish for the last time. Walk the ruins, visit the visitor center, and let the Spanish moss set the mood.

🌊
Jekyll Island

Driftwood Beach

One of the most photographed beaches in the Southeast. Ancient bleached tree trunks emerge from the sand on Jekyll Island's northern tip — haunting, beautiful, and unlike anything else on the East Coast. Perfect for golden-hour photos and morning walks.

🐢
Wildlife & Conservation

Georgia Sea Turtle Center & Releases

Jekyll Island's Georgia Sea Turtle Center is the only sea turtle rehabilitation hospital in the state. Tour the facility, meet recovering turtles, and if you time it right, join a sea turtle release on the beach. During nesting season (May–August), sunrise turtle walks track new nests.

🏡
Exclusive & Fascinating

Biking on Sea Island

The wealthiest zip code in Georgia — average home price north of $5 million. You can't stay there without a reservation at The Cloister, but guests and day visitors can bike the island's pristine roads past the Forbes Five-Star resort and some of the most extraordinary private estates in the South.

🎳
Classic Fun

Putt-Putt at the Pier

A St. Simons tradition — the miniature golf course right at the Pier is a classic for families of any age. Play a round, grab an ice cream, and watch the sunset over St. Simons Sound. Hard to beat.

👻
Night Adventure

Nightly Ghost Tours

With 300 years of history and enough lore to fill a library, St. Simons Island has no shortage of ghost stories. Nightly ghost tours depart from the Pier area and cover the island's most haunted spots — the Lighthouse, the old plantation ruins, and Christ Church cemetery.

🎡
Pier & Village

Pier Village

The social center of St. Simons Island. The Pier juts out into the Atlantic — one of only three in Georgia that extends into the ocean. Pier Village has shops, restaurants, live music venues, and the putt-putt course. The best place to be at night.

"Christ Church — my parents are buried there. Fort Frederica is just an easy walk through history. And when you're standing at the top of that lighthouse, looking out at the Atlantic, you understand why people keep coming back."
🐢

Sea Turtle Season: May–August

Loggerhead sea turtles nest on the island's beaches during summer months. The Georgia Sea Turtle Center at Jekyll Island is the best place to see rescue, rehabilitation, and — if you're lucky — a full release back into the ocean.

⚓

WWII Offshore

German U-boats patrolled just off the Georgia coast during WWII. The Maritime Museum at the Coast Guard Station covers this chapter of the island's history — a surprisingly gripping story that most visitors don't know about.

Get Outside

Biking, Golf & Beach

St. Simons was made for people who want to be outside. 30+ miles of paved bike trails, world-class golf, wide uncrowded beaches, and a conservation-preserved wilderness for hiking — all on an island you can cross in 20 minutes on two wheels.

🚲
The Island Way

30+ Miles of Bike Trails

Wide, paved, well-maintained bike paths run all over the island. You can literally get anywhere — the beach, dinner, the lighthouse, the pier — without touching a car. Rent a beach cruiser or bring your own. This is the definitive way to experience St. Simons.

🛺
Easy & Fun

Golf Cart Rentals

If the bikes aren't enough, rent a golf cart and cruise the island's shaded roads. It's the most leisurely way to explore, and families love it. Check rental shops near the Pier and at several locations around the island.

⛳
Best Course on the Island

Hampton Club Golf Course

The Bias family favorite. Hampton Club is a gorgeous semi-private course on the northern end of St. Simons — marsh views, live oaks, great conditions. Book a tee time well in advance during summer.

⛳
Public Access

Sea Palms Golf Course

The only fully public course on St. Simons Island. Well-maintained, fun layout, good for all skill levels. Sea Palms is your go-to if Hampton Club is booked or you want a more casual round.

🏌️
Budget Round

Jekyll Island Golf Club

Just across the water on Jekyll Island, the Golf Club offers the most affordable links-style course in the Golden Isles. The Great Dunes course has been here since 1898. A fun, breezy, no-pretense round with good views.

🏖️
Room to Breathe

East Beach & Coast Guard Beach

Wide, uncrowded, and beautiful. East Beach and the adjacent Coast Guard Beach area offer ample space — you won't be right on top of neighboring umbrellas. The water is calm, the sand is soft, and the sunrises are worth an early alarm.

🌿
Hiking

Cannon's Point Preserve

644 acres of preserved maritime forest, salt marsh, and ancient history on the northern tip of St. Simons. Hiking and biking trails, kayak access, and ruins dating back 2,500 years to Native American habitation. The best hiking on the island.

🌳
Nature Trail

Guale Preserve

Nearly three miles of trails through maritime forest and old dune ridges — protected by the St. Simons Land Trust. Accessible right from the main bike path on Lawrence Road. Perfect for a quiet morning or evening walk.

🏊
On Sea Island

Biking Sea Island

Five miles of private beach, world-class facilities, and Georgia's wealthiest zip code — Sea Island connects to St. Simons by causeway. Bike the island's roads for one of the most scenic (and humbling) rides on the East Coast. The Cloister is right there if you need a pit stop.

Indulge

Spa & Shopping

St. Simons Island delivers on indulgence without pretension. World-class spas, one-of-a-kind boutiques, and a waterfront shopping district that feels like a well-kept local secret.

✨

The Spa at Sea Island — Forbes Five-Star

Sixty-five thousand square feet of pure indulgence. The Spa at Sea Island's Cloister has earned a Forbes Five-Star rating and has been consistently ranked among the best resort spas in the United States. Signature massages, advanced facials, holistic therapies, personal training, yoga, Pilates — this is the real deal. Access requires a stay at The Cloister or The Lodge, or a day spa reservation as a non-guest.

🌺
Forbes Five-Star

The Cloister Spa, Sea Island

The gold standard. 65,000 sq ft. Massages, facials, body treatments, a labyrinth walk, sound journeys, and the full Forbes Five-Star treatment. If you're going to splurge once on the trip, this is it.

🏖️
On St. Simons

The King and Prince Spa

St. Simons' own spa option at the historic King and Prince Resort. Oceanfront location, full range of treatments. A great choice if you're staying on island and want the spa experience without the Sea Island price tag.

🛍️
Must-Visit

Pier Village & Mallery Street

The waterfront shopping hub of St. Simons Island. One-of-a-kind souvenirs, local boutiques, galleries, bait shops, and everything in between — all within easy walking distance of the Pier and Lighthouse. Best experienced on an evening stroll.

👗
Local Shopping

Redfern Village

St. Simons' other great shopping district — tree-lined streets with locally owned boutiques, art galleries, and specialty shops. Southern Soul BBQ is right here too, so your shopping trip has a natural pit stop built in.

🎨
Art & Gifts

Local Art Galleries

The Golden Isles has a thriving arts community. Galleries throughout Pier Village and Redfern Village feature work by local and regional artists inspired by the coast, the marshes, and the maritime forest. The perfect place to find something to take home that actually means something.

🌿
Sea Island

The Market at Sea Island

Open to the public — a great stop if you're biking Sea Island. The Market features on-the-go sandwiches, specialty food items, snacks, and Sea Island provisions. A beautiful space that doubles as a quick lunch spot.
