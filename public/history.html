Our History Here

Nothing has changed. That's entirely the point.

"I've been coming to St. Simons since I was in diapers — and it looks exactly the same. No high-rise condos. No big hotel chains crowding the skyline. Just live oaks, salt air, and the same easy pace it's always had."

Protected by Law
Georgia prohibits the kind of development that has swallowed every other coastal town. The island looks like it did fifty years ago — on purpose.
Leave Your Keys in the Car
Nobody locks their door here. Strangers wave. You can bike everywhere. It's the kind of Southern living that feels almost impossible to find anymore.
Christ Church
My parents are buried here. Built in 1884 on the same ground where the Wesley brothers preached in 1735. One of the most sacred stops on the island for our family.
Fort Frederica
A National Monument — General Oglethorpe's 1736 fort where the Spanish were turned back for good. You walk through actual ruins on a Tuesday morning. History is casual here.
