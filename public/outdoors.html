The Outdoors

Thirty miles of trails, world-class golf, and a beach that actually has room.

30+ Miles of Bike Trails
Wide, paved, and well-maintained throughout the island. The live-oak canopy on Frederica Road alone is worth the trip. Rent a beach cruiser or bring your own — either way, this is how you experience St. Simons.
Hampton Club Golf Course
My favorite course on the island. Semi-private, northern end of St. Simons, marsh views everywhere. Book early — summer tee times go fast.
Sea Palms Golf Course
The island's only fully public course. Good conditions, fun layout, all skill levels welcome. Your fallback if Hampton Club is booked.
Jekyll Island Golf Club
The most affordable links course in the Golden Isles. The Great Dunes course dates to 1898. Casual, breezy, and great value — especially if you want to play two rounds in a day.
East Beach & Coast Guard Beach
Wide and uncrowded. You will not be right next to a stranger's umbrella. The sandbar, the birds, the sunrise — all worth the walk from wherever you're staying.
Cannon's Point Preserve
644 acres of maritime forest and salt marsh on the northern tip of St. Simons. Best hiking on the island. History goes back 2,500 years — there are ruins in there from Native American habitation.
